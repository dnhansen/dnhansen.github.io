Analysis

  • Calculus 1Autumn 2016
    Differential and integral calculus for functions of one variable. Partial derivatives. Double integrals and iterated integrals over rectangles. Polar coordinates. Convergence of sequences and series. Complex numbers. Matrices and linear maps. Determinants. Linear systems of equations.
    Literature: Stewart, Calculus. Nielsen, Salomonsen, Lineær algebra via eksempler.
  • Calculus 2Autumn 2016
    Directional derivatives, gradients, differentials of functions in several variables. Extrema. Double integrals over general domains and in polar coordinates. Taylor polynomials and series. Examples of solution of differential equations. Eigenvalues and eigenvectors, diagonalisation. Inner product spaces, orthogonal projection.
    Literature: Stewart, Calculus. Nielsen, Salomonsen, Lineær algebra via eksempler.
  • Fourier AnalysisAutumn 2017
    Periodic functions. Orthonormal systems and Fourier series. Pointwise and uniform convergence. Definition of the Fourier integral and its basic properties. Examples and applications.
    Literature: Boggess, Narcowich, A First Course in Wavelets with Fourier Analysis. Lecture notes.
  • Mathematical Analysis 1Autumn 2017
    Naïve set theory, order theory, construction of the real and complex numbers, and the topology of Euclidean space. Sequences and series of real and complex numbers. Basic properties of continuous functions.
    Literature: Poulsen, Funktioner af en og flere variable. Lecture notes.
  • Mathematical Analysis 2Spring 2018
    Uniform continuity. Differentiation and integration of functions of one real variable. Partial derivatives. Sequences and series of functions and the associated concepts of convergence. Power series, Taylor series, Fourier series.
    Literature: Poulsen, Funktioner af en og flere variable. Lecture notes.
  • Complex AnalysisSpring 2019
    Holomorphic functions and the Cauchy–Riemann equations. Contour integrals, antiderivatives, winding numbers, Cauchy’s theorem. Laurent series, isolated singularities, complex logarithms, Cauchy’s residue theorem, Rouche’s theorem.
    Literature: Berg, Complex Analysis.
  • Differential EquationsSpring 2019
    Separation of variables. Transformations of equations. Homogeneous and inhomogeneous systems of linear ordinary differential equations. Asymptotic behaviour of solutions. Existence and uniqueness of solutions to ordinary differential equations.
    Literature: Møller, Ordinary Differential Equations.
  • Theory of Measure and IntegrationAutumn 2019
    Basic measure theory, $\sigma$-algebras and measures. Dynkin systems and uniqueness theorems. Construction of measures, outer measures. The Lebesgue integral. Product measures. Basic properties of $L^p$-spaces. Hilbert spaces. Densities and transformation of measures. The Fourier transform. Fundamental aspects of probability theory.
    Literature: Thorbjørnsen, Grundlæggende mål- og integralteori.
  • Advanced AnalysisSpring 2020
    Topological vector spaces, convexity and weak topologies. Banach algebras and C*-algebras. Spectral theory, in particular of bounded operators on Hilbert space.
    Literature: Rudin, Real and Complex Analysis. Rudin, Functional Analysis. Lecture notes.

Algebra

  • Linear AlgebraSpring 2017
    Systems of linear equations, matrix algebra. Vector spaces linear transformations. Inner product spaces. The method of least squares. Determinants, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, diagonalisation. Symmetric, Hermitian, and normal matrices. Adjoint operators and spectral theorems. The Cayley–Hamilton theorem. Solution of systems of linear differential equations.
    Literature: Lecture notes.
  • Perspectives in MathematicsAutumn 2018
    Bezout’s identity for integers and polynomials, unique factorisation of integers and polynomials. Roots of polynomials and the fundamental theorem of algebra. Elemetary ring theory. Euler’s Theorem. Lagrange interpolation. Concrete construction of the complex numbers and number fields. Reed–Solomon codes, the RSA cryptosystem, Diffie–Hellman key exchange, ElGamal encryption, Shamir’s Secret Sharing, Pollard’s $\rho$ algorithm for logarithms.
    Literature: Hansen, Tal og polynomier.
  • AlgebraAutumn 2019
    Quadratic residues. Group theory including permutation groups and group actions. Ring theory, commutative rings, factorisation in rings. Cyclotomic polynomials. Finite fields. Gröbner bases and systems of polynomial equations.
    Literature: Lauritzen, Concrete Abstract Algebra.

Geometry

  • Introduction to Geometry and TopologyAutumn 2018
    Metric and topological spaces with applications in geometry. Complete metric spaces and Banach’s fixed-point theorem. Topology of metric spaces. Topological spaces, continuity, homeomorphisms and bases. Subspaces and finite products of topological spaces. Hausdorff spaces. Connected and path-connected spaces. Quotient spaces and examples of compact surfaces. Topological graph theory and the Jordan curve theorem.
    Literature: Sutherland, Introduction to Metric and Topological Spaces. Lecture notes.
  • GeometrySpring 2020
    Differential geometry of curves and surfaces in Euclidean space. The fundamental theorem for space curves. The inverse function theorem, the implicit function theorem and Green’s theorem. Global theorems for curves. Regular surfaces in Euclidean space and smooth functions. The first and second fundamental forms. Integration on surfaces. Gaussian curvature. Vector fields and the covariant derivative. Geodesic curves. The Gauss–Bonnet theorem for compact surfaces. Minimal surfaces. Abstract surfaces.
    Literature: do Carmo, Differential Geometry of Curves and Surfaces. Lecture notes.
  • Lie GroupsAutumn 2020
    Smooth manifolds, tangent spaces and smooth maps. Vector fields, integral curves and flows. Integration of densities on manifolds. Lie groups and their Lie algebras, vector fields on Lie groups, and the exponential function. Subgroups of Lie groups. Classification of Lie groups. Homogeneous spaces. Representation theory of Lie groups and Lie algebras. Harmonic analysis on abelian Lie groups, compact Lie groups, and compact homogeneous spaces.
    Literature: Lecture notes.
  • Algebraic TopologyAutumn 2020
    The fundamental group, the van Kampen theorem and covering spaces. Applications to cell complexes. Simplicial, singular and cellular homology. Simplicial approximation. Classical theorems such as the Brouwer fixed point theorem, the Jordan curve theorem, and the Borsuk–Ulam theorem.
    Literature: Hatcher, Algebraic Topology.

Statistics

  • Statistics and Data Reduction (Physics)Spring 2017
    Basic statistical models and methods. Average, empirical variance, confidence intervals, propagation of error, comparing two experiments, and regression models. Analysis of concrete experimental data.
    Literature: Jensen, Statistik – viden fra data.
  • Introduction to Probability Theory and StatisticsAutumn 2018
    Discrete and absolutely continuous random variables. Independence, distributions, mean and variance. Statistical inference, p-values, confidence intervals. Markov chains.
    Literature: Pishro-Nik, Introduction to Probability, Statistics, and Random Processes.
  • Mathematical StatisticsSpring 2019
    Graphical assessment of data and data visualisation. Maximum likelihood estimation and the method of moments. Confidence intervals. Statistical tests including likelihood ratio tests, permutation tests, and tests of independence in contingency tables. Properties of tests such as bias, consistency and power. Bootstrap. Regression analysis, analysis of variance, and general linear models. Simulation and data analysis with R.
    Literature: Chihara, Hesterberg, Mathematical Statistics with Resampling and R. Lecture notes.
  • Advanced Probability TheorySpring 2021
    Literature: Lecture notes.
  • Stochastic ProcessesSpring 2021
    Literature: Lecture notes.
  • Multivariate Statistical AnalysisAutumn 2021
    Literature: Lecture notes.

Misc mathematics

  • Philosophy and Ethics of MathematicsAutumn 2018
    Foundational questions in mathematics. The development of mathematics and mathematical practice. The social organization and social responsibility of mathematics.
    Literature: Johansen, Sørensen, Invitation til matematikkens videnskabsteori.
  • Bachelor Project in MathematicsAutumn 2020
    Unbounded operators on Hilbert space. Properties of symmetric and self-adjoint operators. The Cayley transform and the spectral theorem for self-adjoint operators. One-parameter unitary groups and Stone’s theorem. Applications to quantum mechanics.
    Literature: Various textbooks and articles.

Physics

  • Introductory MechanicsAutumn 2016
    Motion, velocity, and acceleration. Newton’s laws and applications. Work and kinetic energy. Potential energy and energy conservation. Momentum. Motion of rigid bodies. Experimental exercises.
    Literature: Young, Freedman, University Physics.
  • Special Relativity TheoryAutumn 2016
    The Lorentz transformation, time dilation and length contraction, transformation of velocity, Doppler effect and relativistic energy and momentum. The equivalence principle and general relativity. Experimental exercises, e.g. the measurement of the speed of light and the lifetime of the muon.
    Literature: Sartori, Understanding Relativity. Uggerhøj, Speciel relativitetsteori.
  • Mechanics and ThermodynamicsAutumn 2016
    Motion of rigid bodies. Mechanical equilibrium and elasticity. Gravitation, the motion of planets, and Kepler’s laws. Periodic motion, harmonic oscillators and resonance. Fluid mechanics. Temperature and heat, thermal properties of matter. The first and second law of thermodynamics. Experimental exercises.
    Literature: Young, Freedman, University Physics.
  • AstrophysicsAutumn 2016
    Planetary physics and the Solar System. Stellar structure, formation and evolution. Black holes, neutron stars and white dwarfs. Observations of stars, planets and galaxies. The Milky Way and other galaxies. The structure of the Universe. The formation, evolution and expansion of the Universe.
    Literature: Ryden, Peterson, Foundations of Astrophysics.
  • Numerical PhysicsSpring 2017
    Using basic MATLAB or Octave commands. Handling numeric data. Declaring and using MATLAB or Octave functions. Solving equations. Solving differential equations. Writing small programs.
    Literature: Lecture notes.
  • ElectromagnetismSpring 2017
    Electric charge, force and field, Gauss’ law, electric potential, current, resistance, electromotive force, electric circuits, magnetic fields, induction and alternating currents, culminating with the presentation of Maxwell’s equations. Experimental exercises.
    Literature: Young, Freedman, University Physics.
  • Waves and OpticsSpring 2017
    Mechanical waves, superposition of waves, sound waves, electromagnetic waves. Light propagation, geometrical optics, optical instruments. Interference and diffraction. Experimental exercises. Project on microscopic modelling of propagation of mechanical waves.
    Literature: Young, Freedman, University Physics.
  • Quantum MechanicsAutumn 2017
    The wave function and the Schrödinger equation. Representation of observables and rules of interpretation. Simple one-dimensional problems, e.g. square well, tunnelling and the harmonic oscillator. Motion in three dimensions with emphasis on angular momentum and the hydrogen atom. Spin. Identical particles. Time-independent perturbation theory. Historical aspects of quantum mechanics. Project on analytic solution of two-level systems.
    Literature: Griffiths, Introduction to Quantum Mechanics.
  • Advanced MechanicsAutumn 2017
    Calculus of variations and analytical mechanics. Central force problems, mechanics in non-intertial frames, coupled oscillators, and special relativity. Connection between Hamiltonian mechanics and quantum mechanics. Applications of analytical mechanics to classical electrodynamics.
    Literature: Taylor, Classical Mechanics.
  • Atomic and Molecular PhysicsSpring 2018
    The structure of atoms with one and multiple electrons, the periodic table of elements. Interaction between atoms and external fields, in particular static magnetic fields and oscillatory light fields. Selection rules. Mechanisms for molecular binding in diatomic and polyatomic molecules.
    Literature: Foot, Atomic Physics. Demtröder, Atoms, Molecules and Photons.
  • ElectrodynamicsSpring 2018
    Vector analysis. Classical electrodynamics of static and dynamic fields. Electric and magnetic properties of materials and their response to electric, magnetic and electromagnetic fields. Maxwell’s equations in differential form. Electromagnetic waves in vacuum and matter and radiation from an electric dipole. Conversion laws. Applications of gauge theory to electrodynamics.
    Literature: Griffiths, Introduction to Electrodynamics.
  • Experimental Physics 2Spring 2018
    Experimental exercises on the Fresnel relations, emmission and absorption spectra, and Michelson and Mach–Zehnder interferometers.
    Literature: Notes.

Programming

  • Introduction to Programming with Scientific ApplicationsSpring 2019
    Basic procedural and object-oriented programming. Algorithmic techniques, in particular sorting, binary search and dynamic programming. Functional programming. Testing and debugging. Scientific computing in Python. Project on computational geometry.
    Literature: Guttag, Introduction to Computation and Programming Using Python.
  • Software Engineering and ArchitectureAutumn 2021
    Central theories, methods, and techniques for modern software engineering of large software systems with main emphasis on software architecture for flexible, reliable, and testable distributed system. Variability management techniques with emphasis on compositional/responsibility-centric designs, design and architectural patterns, patterns for distribution in the client-server style, as well as frameworks. Agile methods, test-driven development, test doubles, testing levels, test coverage, and systematic testing techniques.
    Literature: Christensen, Flexible and Reliable Software. Christensen, Flexible, Reliable, Distributed Software. Notes and handouts.

Theoretical CS

  • Algorithms and Data StructuresAutumn 2019
    Basic algorithms and data structures. Graph algorithms. Algorithm paradigms, such as divide-and-conquer, greedy algorithms and dynamic programming. Recursive data structures. Analysis of complexity and correctness of algorithms.
    Literature: Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein, Introduction to Algorithms.
  • Computability and LogicSpring 2020
    Regular languages and finite automata. Context-free languages and grammars, parsing, and push-down automata. Recursive languages, Turing machines, termination, decidability and computability. Properties of these language classes, such as equivalence, closure properties, limitations, transformations, and proof techniques, including pumping lemmas, diagonalization, and reduction. Characterizations of decidable and semi-decidable problem classes, Rice’s theorem. Basic computational complexity, including P vs. NP and Cook’s theorem. Introduction to models and proofs for propositional logic, predicate logic, arithmetic, and program logic. Gödel’s completeness and incompleteness theorems.
    Literature: Martin, Introduction to Languages and the Theory of Computation. Huth, Ryan, Logic in Computer Science.

    Literature: Elmasri, Navathe, Fundamentals of Database Systems.
  • Computer Architecture, Networks and Operating SystemsSpring 2022
    Literature: Tanenbaum, Austin, Structured Computer Organization. Hailperin, Operating Systems and Middleware. Dordal, Introduction to Computer Networks.
