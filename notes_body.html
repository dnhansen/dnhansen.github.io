//h2;Notes

I have written a series of notes, kept here as easy references to myself and to whoever might find them useful. See also my list of literature recommendations.

//h3;Mathematics //h4;Analysis and probability theory
  • Conceptual foundations of probability theory. Notes on probability theory from a conceptual perspective. Develops a theory of probability on complete Boolean algebras and studies its relationship with measure-theoretical probability theory. Focuses on the interpretation of constructions like $\sigma$-algebras, independence, and conditional expectation.
  • Unbounded Operators on Hilbert Space with Applications in Quantum Mechanics. My bachelor project in mathematics. Develops the basic theory of unbounded operators, including the spectral theorem for self-adjoint operators and Stone’s theorem. Briefly studies some important operators from quantum mechanics.
  • Miscellaneous analysis notes. Notes on various topics in analysis, including the topology on $\mathbb{R}$, multidimensional differential calculus, the Riemann–Stieltjes integral, line integrals, and notions of convergence in measure theory.
  • Notes on Radon measures
  • Notes on the multivariate normal distribution
  • Notes on Folland’s Real Analysis
  • Notes on Folland’s A Course in Abstract Harmonic Analysis
  • Notes on Ullrich’s Complex Made Simple
  • Notes on Lauritzen’s Undergraduate Convexity
